What we cut
  • Filler words. "um", "uh", "like", "you know", and "I mean" when they don't add meaning.
  • Long silences. Pauses longer than a configurable threshold get tightened.
  • Dead air. Throat clears, gear bumps, false starts that interrupt your flow.
  • Repeated takes. If you said the same sentence three times, we keep the best one.

How the cleanup pass works

AutoCuts transcribes your audio, identifies every word and silence, and applies a cut policy that's tuned for talking-head video. The output isn't a Frankenstein edit, we use frame-accurate cuts with a short audio crossfade, so the result sounds like one take.

You then see a diff view: the original transcript with every removed phrase struck through. If we cut something you wanted to keep, a deliberate pause, a stylistic "um", you click to restore it. The video re-renders in seconds.

What we don't cut

This is the part most AI editors get wrong. Aggressive cleanup makes you sound like a robot. AutoCuts is intentionally conservative:

  • We keep filler words when they carry emphasis ("It was, like, perfect").
  • We keep pauses that follow a question or a key statement, those land harder with breathing room.
  • We never restructure your sentences or change wording.
  • We don't add AI-generated voice. Your voice stays your voice.

What this saves you

The boring math: a 40-minute raw recording typically becomes a 28-minute polished cut, and the cleanup pass alone removes around 600 small edits. If you do this manually, that's two to three hours of scrubbing. AutoCuts does it in under ten minutes, and you spend that time reviewing instead of clicking.
