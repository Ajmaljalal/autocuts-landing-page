How AutoCuts picks the moments

Bad shorts tools chop a long video into evenly-spaced 60-second windows and call it done. AutoCuts is built around a simple observation: the moments that work as shorts have a structure, a hook in the first 3 seconds, a thought that builds, and a payoff that earns the watch.

Our model reads the transcript and audio together. It looks for the lines you said with extra emphasis, the surprising claims, the moments where the energy shifted. It avoids cutting in the middle of a thought, and it never picks a clip that ends on a half-sentence.

Reframe + caption, automatically

Talking-head video shot in 16:9 doesn't fit on TikTok unless you reframe. AutoCuts tracks the speaker through every clip, if you move, the frame follows, so your face stays in the safe zone for every platform's UI overlay.

Captions are burned in (not subtitle tracks) so they survive every platform's compression. You pick a caption style, animated word-by-word, full sentence, highlight-keyword, etc., and it applies to every clip you generate.

You decide what ships

AutoCuts produces suggestions, not decrees. You'll see all the candidate clips in a grid, ranked by how strong we think they are. Swap a clip's start/end by 2 seconds, replace one entirely, kill the ones that don't fit your channel. Cleanup runs automatically on each clip so the audio sounds tight even when you're zoomed in.
